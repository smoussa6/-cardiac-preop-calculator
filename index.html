Subjective METs — Quick Reference

Note: Subjective METs did NOT predict 30-day MI/death in the METS study (Lancet 2018). DASI is the preferred validated tool per 2024 AHA/ACC. This reference is for teaching and clinical approximation.

1 METEat, dress, use toilet
2 METsWalk indoors, light housework
3 METsWalk 1-2 level blocks
4 METsClimb a flight of stairs, walk uphill, heavy housework
5-6 METsBrisk walk (4 mph), golf, dance
7-8 METsJog, singles tennis
≥10 METsRun, swim laps, strenuous sports
Key landmark: ≥4 METs (climb a flight of stairs) is the traditional threshold for "adequate" functional capacity. However, 2024 guidelines recommend using DASI for more accurate risk prediction.

📖 Teaching Point

The subjective METs scale remains useful for bedside estimation and teaching. However, the METS trial (n=1,401) showed that physician-estimated METs did not independently predict 30-day MI or death, while DASI score did (OR 0.96 per unit, p=0.009).

Use DASI for documentation and risk stratification. Use the METs scale as a quick mental reference.

Cardiac Biomarkers

2024 AHA/ACC: NT-proBNP Class 2a, hs-troponin Class 2b for perioperative risk stratification

Indicated when: age ≥65, known CVD, or age ≥45 with cardiac symptoms.

Thresholds: NT-proBNP ≥125 pg/mL (age <75) or ≥450 pg/mL (age ≥75). Normal biomarkers in indicated patients obviate further cardiac testing.
