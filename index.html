Subjective METs — Quick Reference

Subjective METs did NOT predict 30-day MI/death in METS study (Lancet 2018). For teaching/bedside use only.

1 METEat, dress, use toilet
2 METsWalk indoors, light housework
3 METsWalk 1-2 level blocks
4 METsClimb a flight of stairs, walk uphill
5-6 METsBrisk walk, golf, dance
7-8 METsJog, singles tennis
≥10 METsRun, swim laps
Key landmark: ≥4 METs (climb stairs) = traditional "adequate" threshold. 2024 guidelines recommend DASI.

📖 Teaching Point

METS trial (n=1,401): physician-estimated METs did not independently predict 30-day MI or death. DASI did (OR 0.96/unit, p=0.009). Use DASI for documentation, METs for bedside reference.

ℹ️ About This Tool

A perioperative cardiovascular risk stratification tool designed for inpatient preoperative consultation. Generates a copy-paste-ready assessment and plan for the EMR.

📊 Included Scores

  • RCRI — 30-day MACE (Lee 1999, Davis 2013)
  • AUB-HAS2 — 30-day death/MI/stroke (Dakik 2019)
  • DASI — validated functional capacity (Hlatky 1989, METS 2018)
  • Cardiac Biomarkers — NT-proBNP/hs-troponin (2024 AHA/ACC)

📖 Guideline Basis

2024 AHA/ACC Guideline for Perioperative Cardiovascular Management for Noncardiac Surgery (Circulation 2024;150:e351)

🔒 Privacy

100% client-side. No data transmitted, stored, or logged. No analytics, cookies, or API calls.
